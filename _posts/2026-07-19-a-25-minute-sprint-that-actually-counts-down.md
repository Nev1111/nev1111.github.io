Sharp-eyed readers noticed the sidebar has always shown a **workflow sprint** clock stuck at 25:00. As of today, it works. Click the clock to start, click again to pause, double-click to reset. It keeps running as you move around the site.

## Why 25 minutes

A workflow sprint is one focused block spent making a *recurring* task better instead of just doing it again. Twenty-five minutes is short enough to fit before a meeting and long enough to produce something real:

- define the source, the rule, the exception, and the review output for one task
- write the control-total check for one close workbook
- draft one procedure as a `SKILL.md` your AI agent could run
- turn one exception report into matched / source-A-only / source-B-only buckets

Each sprint comes with a mission like these — reset the clock and you'll get a new one.

## The rules

1. One task. Not your whole close.
2. Write something down. A rule, a check, a skill draft — the artifact is the point.
3. When the clock hits zero, stop and post what you finished in the [Discord]({{ '/community/' | relative_url }}).

## Accounting takeaway

Improvement doesn't need a project plan; it needs a repeatable 25-minute habit. One sprint a week is 50+ documented improvements a year — and every one of them is a candidate for the [Skills Library]({{ '/skills/' | relative_url }}).
