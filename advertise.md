PANDAUDIT should feel like a field guide people want to share: useful, slightly playful, and grounded in real finance work.

## Who to reach

Not accountants alone. The best audience is anyone who touches messy business data:

- FP&A analysts and finance managers
- auditors and advisory teams
- controllers and accounting managers
- operations analysts
- revenue, billing, and AR/AP teams
- treasury and cash operations
- students trying to become more data-fluent
- Excel power users who know there has to be a better way

## Where to promote

### LinkedIn

Post short before/after examples:

> “The spreadsheet problem: three exports, no shared naming convention, and a deadline. The analytics concept: standardize fields, match by rule, produce exceptions.”

Best formats:

- carousel: messy export → cleanup checklist → exception report
- one-minute screen recording
- poll: “Which month-end task wastes the most time?”
- weekly “field note” thread

### Discord and communities

Use Discord as the workshop, not just a chat room:

- weekly spreadsheet rescue prompt
- “what would you check first?” puzzle
- anonymized workflow teardown
- office-hours thread for finance data problems

### Search and evergreen content

Write posts around the job-to-be-done:

- how to reconcile two reports
- how to clean exported numbers
- how to document an exception report
- how to stop rebuilding the same pivot every month
- how to check data quality before dashboarding

### Partnerships

Look for people who already teach Excel, finance careers, FP&A, audit, or small-business operations. Offer one practical guest post or challenge prompt.

## Fun content ideas

- **The Messy File Museum** — screenshots/mockups of ugly exports and how to reason through them
- **Exception Report Bingo** — duplicates, missing IDs, bad dates, weird credits, blank departments
- **Finance Workflow Boss Levels** — Level 1 cleanup, Level 2 matching, Level 3 controls, Level 4 repeatable workpaper
- **Friday Field Note** — one tiny workflow lesson before the weekend
- **Spreadsheet Confession Booth** — anonymous “I still copy/paste this every month” prompts
